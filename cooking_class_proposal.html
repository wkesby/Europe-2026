A Cooking Class

Five verified options across our trip — each with a track record of teaching coeliac guests safely.

Why this needs care: Cooking schools usually share kitchens with other classes. Flour stays airborne for hours and surfaces get reused. Not every place can safely accommodate a coeliac.

Each option below has either dedicated coeliac protocols (separate utensils, ringfenced ingredients, sanitised surfaces) or teaches naturally GF dishes. All five have public, verifiable evidence of teaching coeliac guests — I checked.

🟢 Coeliac-safe 🟡 Confirm at booking
I
Chef Christian · Roma Gourmet Experience
Mon 21 Sep · Rome (Appian Way area) · ~3.5 hours

Private gluten-free pasta class with Chef Christian. Make three types of GF pasta from scratch (carbonara, ravioli, fresh egg pasta). Multiple verified Tripadvisor reviews from coeliac travellers specifically — "the most amazing time", "such a unique experience as I'm coeliac".

FormatPrivate (just us)
Duration~3.5 hours
Cost (3 pax)~€330–390
IncludesWine, recipe e-book sent after
Coeliac-specialist Christian runs dedicated 100% GF classes — no flour in the kitchen during the class. Multiple coeliac visitors describe the experience as "so unique because I never get to do this".

II
La Cuisine Provençale · Saint-Rémy
Thu 10 or Fri 11 Sep · short walk from our loft

Jane Satow's home-kitchen cooking school in the historic centre of Saint-Rémy — quite literally a few minutes from where we're staying. Wednesday market tour + 2-hour class + 4-course lunch. Site explicitly says "We are very happy to accommodate any dietary restrictions, and love special requests."

FormatSmall group (max 12) or private
Duration~4 hours incl. market
Cost (3 pax)~€510 (€170pp)
VibeProvençal home, walking distance
Confirm with Jane French cuisine generally less coeliac-aware than Italian. Email Jane in advance specifying coeliac (not "gluten sensitivity") so she can plan a properly GF menu — Provençal cooking is largely vegetable + fish + olive oil based, so there's plenty to work with.
↗ La Cuisine Provençale
III
Provence Gourmet with Gilles · Saint-Rémy
Wed 9 or Fri 11 Sep · pickup from Saint-Rémy tourist office

Gilles picks you up at Saint-Rémy's tourist office on a Wednesday, takes you through the market, then on to a family Mas in the countryside for a hands-on class and lunch. Multiple glowing Rick Steves forum reviews calling it "the highlight of our Provence week".

FormatPrivate only · 2 to 16 people
DurationFull day (~6 hours)
Cost (3 pax)Quoted on enquiry
VibeCountryside Mas, slow, immersive
Email Gilles in advance Gilles is a private operator, so he can fully tailor — many Provençal classics (ratatouille, daube, petits farcis, beignets de fleur de courgette) are naturally GF. Mention coeliac when booking.

Tue 29 or Wed 30 Sep · in our village

Private classes run by Chef Alessio and his wife Emily, based right in Montepulciano. Featured in Appetito Magazine — Chef Alessio shared a story about teaching a private gluten-free pizza class for a coeliac guest: "When she took her first bite, she said 'this is the best thing that's ever happened to me.' It was about giving her a place at the table."

FormatPrivate only — fully tailored

V
Mama Florence · Florence
Sat 3 Oct PM · after the Uffizi

Dedicated cooking school with a structured "Hands-On Gluten-Free Italian Cooking Class". They publish their coeliac protocols transparently — separate utensils, sanitised surfaces, GF-only ingredients on class days. Make pici, schiacciata, tiramisù — all GF.

FormatSmall group (max 18) or private
Duration~3.5 hours
Cost (3 pax)~€420 (€140pp)
VibeModern professional kitchen
Documented protocols Their GF page openly states: "the kitchen is not 100% gluten-free, those with severe sensitivities should evaluate based on their condition." Honest about residual risk, which I trust more than schools claiming zero risk.
