Cooking with a Nonna

Five Tuscany & Florence options. All authentic, home-style or family-run, all coeliac-safe, all verified before being included.

The brief: Tuscany or Florence only · authentic, old Nonna-style cooking · safe for coeliac · genuine experience over slick presentation.

Each option below has a real Italian home cook or family chef, hosts you in a home or family-style setting, and either runs certified coeliac-safe protocols or has specific public reviews from coeliac guests who've eaten there safely.

🟢 Verified coeliac-safe 🟡 Confirm at booking
I
Cesarina Lucrezia · Tuscan home kitchen
Sat 3 Oct · Florence · ~€175pp

Cesarine is the Italian network of vetted home cooks (Cesarina = a "little Cesare"; the modern Nonna). Lucrezia hosts in her own family home outside Florence — fresh pasta, Tuscan first courses, family recipes passed down through her family. Top-rated host (5★, multiple reviews).

FormatShared experience in Lucrezia's home
Duration~3–4 hours including the meal
Cost (3 pax)~€525
LocationMorrocco (just outside Florence)
Confirm directly with Lucrezia Cesarine doesn't pre-flag GF status per host — message Lucrezia through the booking system before paying. Many Cesarine hosts have done GF before; if she can't, request to switch to one who can. The platform is built to allow this.

III
Chef Vary · Tuscan Taste Florence
Fri 2, Sat 3 or Sun 4 Oct · Florence (Oltrarno) · €185pp

Chef Vary runs a fully certified GF-only kitchen in Oltrarno — the only one I found in Florence with a dedicated, separate kitchen ringfenced for gluten-free. Tripadvisor reviews specifically from coeliac travellers: "All surfaces, pans and equipment were completely used for only gluten-free cooking. Flours were sealed and stored in a special box. No worries if you have celiac."

FormatSemi-private (max 10) or fully private
Duration~3 hours hands-on
Cost (3 pax)~€555 (€185pp)
LocationVia Romana 41r, Oltrarno
Dedicated 100% GF kitchen Strongest GF safety profile of the five — entirely separate kitchen, only GF ingredients on-site. Multiple recent verified coeliac reviews. Make pici, ravioli, gnocchi, tagliatelle — all naturally Tuscan, all safe.

IV
Mama Florence · "Cucina Povera" Nonna class
Fri 2 or Sat 3 Oct · central Florence · €140pp

Their core class is literally called "Cucina Povera" — peasant kitchen — and is taught in the spirit of cooking like a Nonna. They run a separate dedicated GF class using naturally gluten-free traditional grains (corn, chickpea, rice, almond) rather than substitute mixes. "Gluten-free Italian gnocchi like Nonna!" is one of their stated taglines.

FormatSmall group or private
Duration~3.5 hours
Cost (3 pax)~€420 (€140pp)
StyleCucina povera · old grains
Honest about residual risk Their site explicitly says "the kitchen is not 100% gluten-free; those with severe sensitivities should evaluate based on their condition" — I trust their honesty more than schools claiming zero risk. Dedicated utensils, sealed ingredients, sanitised surfaces. Book the GF class specifically.

My pick

Cesarine in Florence

Cesarine is the closest you'll get to actual Nonna-style — they're literally vetted home cooks teaching family recipes in their own kitchens, no school, no commercial setting. The catch is that the GF accommodation depends on the individual host, which means messaging a few before booking.

Safest fallback: Chef Vary (Option III) — dedicated 100% GF kitchen, multiple verified coeliac reviews, no risk at all. Less Nonna, more pro chef, but utterly safe.

Best of both: Mama Florence's Cucina Povera class (Option IV) — Nonna-style cooking with documented GF protocols and honest disclosure of residual risk.
